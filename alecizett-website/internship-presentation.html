2023 Summer Internship

The Idea

  • This project resulted in a time save of 2 hours per projector per year. The idea of this project is to update a legacy codebase to support a color correction algorithm utilizing the xyY theoretical color space. (I will use "LUV" color space somewhat interchangeably with "xyY" color space.)

Background

Over the summer, I worked at FlightSafety International, a flight simulation company that develops fully immersive simulators. I was part of a team working in a lab setup similar to the one depicted in the picture to the left. Our setup consisted of three projectors with three displays. Our primary responsibility was to provide automated tools for aligning and stitching these displays together when installing simulators in the field.

The Issue?

These projectors are shipped from the manufacturer with slight variances in tolerance, leading to differences in color and brightness (luminance). My role during the summer was to address these tolerance variances by adjusting the projectors' color and luminance settings.

Prerequisite (CIELUV & xyY Color Space)

The essence of the project revolves around the colorspace employed by both new and upcoming projectors. These projectors utilize the xyY color space, which can be thought of as just another method for defining color (similar to RGB). The xyY color space distinguishes color from luminance, a concept that makes much more sense in the context of projectors.
